NerdWallet AI

Context

NerdWallet’s mission is to empower users with the information they need to make life’s financial decisions with confidence. The rise of LLMs presented both a challenge and an opportunity to advance this mission. I led a hackathon team to create an MVP of a chat module incorporating ChatGPT technology, winning the top award and inspiring leadership to establish a dedicated AI team, where I led design.

Objectives

  • User Needs: Provide answers to complex financial questions quickly and accurately.
  • Business Needs: Differentiate NerdWallet from LLM competitors with a unique, trustworthy chat experience.
  • Hypothesis: Integrating a chat experience that answers user queries and directs them to fact-checked NerdWallet content will create a uniquely reliable service.

User Research

I designed and conducted extensive user research studies, including surveys, preference tests, and video-recorded prototype tests with early design iterations. Collaboration with product management is essential to design research that accurately pinpoints user pain points and needs.

Key Insights

  • Usefulness: Users struggle with information overload from lengthy articles. They value time and efficiency, making an AI chat that provides quick, precise answers highly desirable.
  • Trustworthiness: Users are skeptical of LLMs. Linking responses to NerdWallet’s expert-written content helps build trust.
  • Usefulness: Amidst NerdWallet's vast content, the chat needs to stand out without overwhelming the user interface.
  • Discoverability: Users struggle with information overload from lengthy articles. They value time and efficiency, making an AI chat that provides quick, precise answers highly desirable.
  • Clarity: Users often confuse AI chats with traditional chatbots. Clearly signaling the advanced capabilities of the chat is crucial.

Synthesis

Collaborating closely with product, engineering, and design teams, I synthesized research findings into actionable insights, sharing these through detailed research decks. This process ensured cross-functional alignment and buy-in.

Requirements

I partnered with engineering and product teams to define project requirements, milestones, and pathways, ensuring efficient time management and resource allocation. Regular syncs with key stakeholders kept expectations realistic and aligned.

DESIGN

user journey

Clearly defining and visualizing the user journey helps scope the project correctly, design holistically, and anticipate edge cases.

We saw that the chat could be an entry-point for users, help them discover products through NerdWallet and compare products in new and customized ways, and build customer loyalty by driving registrations for premium chat features.

Ideation

I begin lo-fi to wrap my head around the problem space and explore design variants quickly, going wide before going deep. I also collaborate closely with content strategy so that their insight can inform our design direction early on. Using AI tools can help broaden the range of variants we explore.

Competitor analysis

Taking stock of the competitive landscape helps us identify niche opportunities and find design inspiration. After studying existing LLM interfaces, and direct competitors like Parthean and Moneymade, we discovered that NerdWallet could position itself uniquely by providing AI responses enhanced with fact-checked content written by our authoritative NerdWallet experts

Collaboration

Bringing in feedback from product partners and stakeholders early on can yield valuable insights. As a designer, it’s my goal to be seen as low-ego and un-protective of my work, actively seeking out candid feedback so that partners feel at liberty to bring their best thoughts to the table.

Visual refinement

I polish visuals by considering design principles like visual hierarchy, simplicity, and unity, as well as ensuring that the designs will transition seamlessly across all breakpoints.

Prototypes

Prototyping is an absolute must to get a feel for flow, optimize interactions, and test workflows. Sharing prototypes usually evokes more informed and invested feedback.

Research

Before handoff, pressure testing designs using user testing to illuminate usability issues before investing in development time.

Handoff

To ensure seamless translation of design to dev, I thoroughly annotate designs, comprehensively enumerate design requirements, and conduct walk-throughs for the engineers. Putting the work in up-front helps reduce the number of issues that surface in post-development design reviews.

Post-Development

Analysis

Post-development, I used user research studies to qualitatively assess the success of our designs. This supplemented the quantitative data we used to measure engagement. It’s critical to employ user research at the end of the design process as well as at the beginning in order to test hypotheses and get a more tactile sense of how to improve for the future.

conclusion

Successes

Our quantitative data and qualitative user research gave us validation that we had succeeded in using NerdWallet AI to aid the user journey for NerdWallet users and solve the pain points we had identified

User satisfaction

Users reported a high level of satisfaction with NerdWallet AI, remarking on its intuitive usability, speed, and the convenience with which it led them to relevant and trustworthy content

User engagement

Through multiple rounds of research, analysis, and iteration, we increased the engagement with our chatbot from 1.42% to 5.01%.

Boosting the user journey

We discovered that the presence of the chat-bot could boost user engagment with the rest of the site, increasing average user page views and increasing likelihod of monetizing.

Product expansion

Initially, we rolled the chat out on articles as a supplement to the user's reading experience. After seeing success, this expanded to a prominent placement on the home page. Most recently, we moved to include personalization capability by integrating the chat with user's bank account information.
