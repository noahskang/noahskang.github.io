A curated collection of resources exploring the unique challenges Asian Americans face around loneliness, dating, and connection — and the distinctive ways Asian cultures approach these universal human experiences.

3× less likelyto seek mental health support (APA)
45% less likelyto receive mental health treatment (HHS)
29%of Asian Americans live in multigenerational households — highest of any group (Pew 2022)

Loneliness, connection, and belonging look different across cultures — and the Asian American experience brings its own particular textures to each. Research points to real structural barriers around mental health access and help-seeking. Cultural patterns around family, obligation, and emotional expression shape how loneliness is experienced and named. And navigating identity between cultures adds layers that mainstream conversations about connection often don't account for.

At the same time, Asian cultures bring genuine strengths to these challenges — multigenerational bonds, strong community ties, and a growing generation of advocates actively reshaping the conversation. The articles, research, and organizations below don't tell one story. They offer a range of perspectives worth sitting with.

A note from the Asian at Hinge ERG

At Hinge, we build products in service of the belief that human connection is one of the most meaningful things in a person's life — and that loneliness is a problem worth taking seriously. Understanding the specific barriers that different communities face when it comes to vulnerability and connection isn't peripheral to that work. It's central to it.

The resources on this page don't represent Hinge's official positions — they're things our ERG members have found valuable, honest, and worth sharing. If you're navigating any of this yourself, you're not alone, and you don't have to have it all together.
