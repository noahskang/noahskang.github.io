A survey of motion — thirteen studies

Motion, shape, and reaction
prompted into being.

A small gallery of animation studies built in single prompts. Each panel shows a different class of motion — from blobby morphing loaders, to reactive shaders, to Hingie himself losing a fight with an arrow — to give a feel for the range of animation work a frontier model can generate end-to-end.

01 — squash & stretch

Hingie hops

Hingie — Hinge's fuzzy mascot — bouncing up and down like he just had too much coffee. Squash on landing, stretch on takeoff, shadow that squeezes on impact.

Classic squash/stretch on the body group, with an inverse bobble on the eyes so they lag the body. A radial shadow scales horizontally on ground contact. Small dust puffs flick outward when he lands.
LOOPING
02 — confusion

Hingie is confused

Hingie has no idea what's going on. Big question mark on his chest, eyes darting around, little question marks floating up out of his fur.

Head tilts slowly side to side with an ease-in-out curve. Pupils dart to four corners on a faster loop. Three floating ?s staggered on the same float-up keyframe.

03 — narrative loop

Hingie meets arrow

Dark scene. Arrow flies in from the side, lodges itself in Hingie. Eyes go X, he tips over, fur puffs out. Beat. Reset.

The loop is orchestrated across four synchronized 4-second timelines (arrow flight, body rotation, eye swap, puff particles). The stuck arrow is parented to Hingie so it rotates with him when he falls.
LOOPING
04 — interaction

Hingie feels love

Click Hingie and he gets overwhelmed with affection. Heart blooms from his chest, eyes turn into hearts, a flurry of smaller hearts float up around him.

Click-driven. The .active class on the stage triggers six animations at once — body shimmy, heart burst, eye swap, and six staggered floating hearts on individual vectors. Resets after 1500ms.
CLICK HINGIE

07 — canvas / shader

Interactive wallpaper

Imagine a wallpaper for a futuristic operating system. It should feel interactive and fun to fidget with — react to mouse position, and maybe clicks.

Three modes, each a different 2D-canvas field. Cursor warps the field; clicks drop a ripple.

08 — particles

Cosmic drift

Create a particle field that rolls through space and occasionally whispers a fun fact about the distances and sizes of celestial bodies.

Parallax three-layer starfield on canvas. Nearby particles connect with faint lines; a rotating fact-feed cycles every few seconds.

Line drawing

Make a row of icons that draw themselves in, as if hand-sketched. Natural, slightly playful timing.

12 — force field

Magnetic dots

A field of dots that lean toward the cursor as it moves, like iron filings near a magnet.

Grid of 20×12 dots. Each dot computes its vector to the cursor on mousemove and translates proportionally to 1/distance².

Elastic cards
